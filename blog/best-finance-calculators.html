For Homebuyers

Calculators for Homebuyers and Borrowers

Buying a home is the largest financial commitment most people will ever make. Even a small difference in interest rate or loan term can add up to tens of thousands of dollars over the life of your mortgage. These calculators help you see the real cost before you sign anything.

1. Mortgage Calculator

Before you start touring houses, you need to know what you can actually afford. The Mortgage Calculator takes your home price, down payment, interest rate, and loan term, then breaks down your monthly payment into principal, interest, property tax, insurance, and PMI. It even generates a full amortization schedule so you can see exactly how much of each payment goes toward building equity versus paying interest.

Pro tip: Try adjusting the down payment slider to see how putting down 20% eliminates PMI entirely. On a $400,000 home, skipping PMI can save you $150 to $300 per month.

Try Mortgage Calculator →

2. APR Calculator

The interest rate your lender quotes is not the full picture. The APR Calculator factors in origination fees, closing costs, discount points, and other charges to show you the true annual cost of borrowing. This makes it far easier to compare loan offers from different lenders on an equal footing.

Pro tip: When comparing two mortgage offers, always compare APR rather than the quoted interest rate. A loan with a lower rate but higher fees can actually cost more over time.

Try APR Calculator →

3. EMI Calculator

The EMI Calculator (Equated Monthly Installment) is essential for anyone taking out a personal loan, auto loan, or any fixed-rate installment loan. Enter the loan amount, interest rate, and tenure, and you get your exact monthly obligation plus a visual breakdown of total interest versus principal paid.

Pro tip: Use this calculator to experiment with shorter loan tenures. Cutting a 5-year car loan to 3 years increases your monthly payment, but the total interest saved is often significant enough to justify the stretch.

Try EMI Calculator →

4. Loan Comparison Calculator

Deciding between two or three loan offers? The Loan Comparison Calculator lets you enter the details of multiple loans side by side and instantly see which one costs less in total interest, which has the lowest monthly payment, and which lets you pay off the balance fastest. It removes the guesswork from one of the most consequential financial decisions you will make.

Pro tip: Do not just compare monthly payments. A loan with a lower monthly payment but a longer term almost always costs more in total. Focus on total cost of borrowing.

Try Loan Comparison →

For Investors

Calculators for Investors and Savers

Whether you are building an emergency fund, saving for your child's education, or growing a retirement nest egg, understanding how your money grows (or shrinks) over time is critical. These calculators put the power of compound math in your hands without requiring a finance degree.

5. Compound Interest Calculator

Albert Einstein reportedly called compound interest the eighth wonder of the world, and once you see the numbers, you will understand why. The Compound Interest Calculator shows you how an initial investment grows over time with regular contributions and compounding. Adjust the compounding frequency (daily, monthly, quarterly, or annually) to see exactly how your money multiplies.

Pro tip: Start early. Investing $200 per month starting at age 25 at a 7% annual return grows to roughly $525,000 by age 65. Waiting until 35 to start the same contributions yields only about $244,000. The ten-year head start nearly doubles your result.

Try Compound Interest Calculator →

6. ROI Calculator

The ROI Calculator (Return on Investment) helps you evaluate whether an investment was worth it. Enter what you paid, what you received, and the time period, and you get your net profit, percentage return, and annualized ROI. It works for stocks, real estate, business ventures, or even that vintage guitar you bought five years ago.

Pro tip: Always calculate annualized ROI when comparing investments held for different time periods. A 50% return over five years is actually less impressive than a 20% return over one year on an annualized basis.

Try ROI Calculator →

7. Inflation Calculator

A dollar today will not buy the same amount ten years from now. The Inflation Calculator shows you the real purchasing power of your money over time by factoring in historical or projected inflation rates. This is invaluable for long-term financial planning, salary negotiations, and understanding whether your investment returns are actually beating inflation.

Pro tip: When planning for retirement, always think in real (inflation-adjusted) terms. If your investments return 8% per year but inflation averages 3%, your real return is closer to 5%. The inflation calculator helps you see this clearly.

Try Inflation Calculator →

8. Retirement Calculator

The Retirement Calculator is your big-picture planning tool. Enter your current age, savings, monthly contributions, expected return, and desired retirement age, and it projects whether you are on track to retire comfortably. It accounts for inflation, Social Security estimates, and different withdrawal strategies to give you a realistic picture of your financial future.

Pro tip: Run the calculator with both optimistic and conservative return assumptions (say 8% and 5%). If your plan works under the conservative scenario, you are in solid shape. If it only works with optimistic returns, consider increasing your savings rate.

Try Retirement Calculator →
For Business Owners

Calculators for Business Owners and Entrepreneurs

Running a business means making financial decisions every single day. From pricing your products to understanding when your startup will turn profitable, these calculators give you the clarity to make data-driven choices instead of relying on gut feeling.

9. Break-Even Calculator

Every business needs to know its break-even point: the exact number of units you need to sell (or revenue you need to generate) to cover all your costs. The Break-Even Calculator takes your fixed costs, variable cost per unit, and selling price per unit, then shows you the magic number where revenue meets expenses. It also generates a visual chart so you can see how quickly profit grows after you cross that threshold.

Pro tip: Run a break-even analysis before launching any new product or service. If the break-even point requires selling more units than your market realistically supports, you need to revisit your pricing or cost structure before investing further.

Try Break-Even Calculator →

10. Profit Margin Calculator

Healthy margins keep businesses alive. The Profit Margin Calculator computes your gross margin, operating margin, and net margin from your revenue and cost figures. Understanding the difference between a 30% gross margin and a 10% net margin helps you identify where your money is actually going and whether your pricing strategy is sustainable.

Pro tip: Track your profit margins monthly, not just annually. A slowly declining margin is a warning sign that costs are creeping up or pricing power is eroding, both of which are easier to fix when caught early.

Try Profit Margin Calculator →

For Everyday Life

Calculators for Everyday Financial Decisions

Not every financial calculation involves six-figure numbers. These everyday calculators help you handle the small but frequent money decisions that add up over the course of a year.

11. Salary Calculator

Whether you are evaluating a job offer, switching from hourly to salaried pay, or just curious about your effective hourly rate, the Salary Calculator converts between annual, monthly, biweekly, weekly, daily, and hourly pay in seconds. It also factors in common deductions and taxes to show you a more realistic take-home number.

Pro tip: When comparing job offers in different cities, convert both salaries to hourly rates and then factor in cost of living. A $90,000 salary in a high-cost city might actually deliver less spending power than $70,000 in a more affordable area.

Try Salary Calculator →

12. Tip Calculator

Splitting the bill after a group dinner should not require a math degree. The Tip Calculator takes your bill total, desired tip percentage, and number of people, then tells each person exactly what they owe. It handles custom tip amounts and rounds to the nearest dollar if you prefer clean numbers.

Pro tip: A quick mental shortcut for 20% tip: move the decimal point one place left (that gives 10%), then double it. But when splitting among a group or doing custom percentages, let the calculator handle it.

Try Tip Calculator →

13. Percentage Calculator

The Percentage Calculator is a surprisingly versatile tool. It handles three core operations: finding a percentage of a number (what is 15% of 230?), finding what percentage one number is of another (45 is what percent of 200?), and finding the whole when you know a part and a percentage. It is useful for discounts, tax calculations, grade calculations, and a hundred other daily scenarios.

Pro tip: Use the percentage change mode to quickly calculate year-over-year growth rates for your income, expenses, or investments.

Try Percentage Calculator →

14. Sales Tax Calculator

Sales tax rates vary wildly depending on your state, county, and even city. The Sales Tax Calculator lets you quickly compute the tax amount and total price for any purchase. Enter the pre-tax price and your local tax rate, and it shows you the exact total. You can also work backwards: enter the total and it tells you the pre-tax price and tax amount.

Pro tip: If you sell products online, use this calculator to double-check the sales tax you are charging customers in different states. Getting sales tax wrong can lead to compliance headaches down the road.

Try Sales Tax Calculator →

15. Debt Payoff Calculator

Carrying debt from credit cards, student loans, or personal loans? The Debt Payoff Calculator helps you build a realistic payoff plan. Enter your balances, interest rates, and what you can afford to pay each month, and it shows you exactly when you will be debt-free. It compares the avalanche method (highest interest first) with the snowball method (smallest balance first) so you can pick the strategy that works for your psychology and your wallet.

Pro tip: Even an extra $50 per month toward your highest-interest debt can shave months or years off your payoff timeline. Use the calculator to see the exact impact before committing.

Try Debt Payoff Calculator →

Quick Tips for Smarter Financial Calculations

Having great calculators is only half the battle. Here are some practical principles to keep in mind when running the numbers on any financial decision:

  • Always account for inflation. A dollar today is worth more than a dollar in ten years. When planning long-term, use the Inflation Calculator to convert future values into today's dollars.
  • Run multiple scenarios. Do not settle on one set of assumptions. Change the interest rate, investment return, or monthly contribution by a few percentage points and see how the outcome shifts. The range of results tells you more than a single number ever could.
  • Compare total cost, not monthly payment. Lenders love to focus on monthly payments because they sound manageable. Use the Mortgage Calculator or Loan Comparison to see total interest paid over the full term.
  • Separate emotions from math. The numbers do not lie. If a car loan stretches to seven years just to make the payment affordable, the calculator is telling you the car is too expensive, not that the loan is a good deal.
  • Revisit your calculations regularly. Your financial situation changes over time. Re-run your Retirement Calculator and Debt Payoff Calculator every six months to make sure your plan still fits your reality.
